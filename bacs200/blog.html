The Future of Digital Commerce: Is it Crypto?

Since the beginning of 2020 and throughout the pandemic, cryptocurrency has been a hot topic. Here is a great place to learn about the pros, cons, and general information revolving around crypto. With the market in its infancy stages, there is constant change occurring throughout the market, keep up here with what is happening in crypto.

Economic Implications

Web 3.0 may change the way people interact and go about their daily lives. With the metaverse (a decentralized-virtual world where individuals interact with one another) growing as quickly as it has, it is no wonder people are so intrigued by the potential of crypto. What does this have to do with these currencies you may ask? Blockchain and cryptocurrencies are the two entities that facilitate the operations of Web 3 and NFTs. Both are ways to interact socially with people worldwide. Crypto allows people to buy, sell, and trade coins, NFTs, and even tangible objects to one another.

Social Implications

Over the last five years crypto has started to become more main stream with the number of investors in the market. As of Jan. 2017, there had been 11.68 million wallets on the blockchain.com. Five years later looking at the same period of this year, there are were 81.21 million users on the same platform (Statista, 2022). This is a major increase in users, and blockchain.com is just one wallet available in the market. Imagine the amount of other accounts on other platforms have been created during that time. More people are beginning to adopt the technology, as well as companies. Use of crypto in B2C transactions is also becoming a reality. Companies such as – Overstock, AT&T, and Starbucks, just to name a few are some of the entities that accept payment of crypto.

Political Implications

In relation to the political implications surrounding these currencies are becoming less decentralized and more regulated by government officials. The United States signed an executive order on March 9th, earlier in the year looking to implement a “whole-of-government approach” to regulate these coins. This order included 6 areas focus to keep investors safe and maintain competition in the market. Increasing regulation of this market may deter investors, but the increase appeal of safety might lead to new buyers whom were once hesitant to enter the market. While the US is increasing regulation, other nations are adopting some cryptos as their country’s currency. El Salvador for example has recently began using Bitcoin as the national tender.

Conclusion

Cryptocurrencies have been a hot topic for the last few years. With an increase in users worldwide, it is no wonder there is so much buzz surrounding the market. It will be interesting to see where the crypto market is heading with concerns to regulation, growth, and development/applications of the technology.
